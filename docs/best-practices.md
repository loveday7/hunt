# Best Practices for Job Search Success

## Cold Emailing Strategy

### Do's ✅

- **Research the company** before sending - mention specific details
- **Personalize heavily** - use templates as guides only
- **Keep it brief** - 3-4 paragraphs maximum
- **Lead with value** - what you bring, not what you want
- **Be specific** - mention concrete achievements with numbers
- **Include clear CTA** - "Would love a quick call this week"
- **Follow up** - once after 1 week if no response
- **Use professional email** - not a generic Gmail
- **Proofread** - typos kill your chances
- **Include resume** - as PDF attachment

### Don'ts ❌

- **Generic copy-paste** - hiring managers can tell
- **Too long emails** - 300-400 words is sweet spot
- **Asking for a job directly** - suggest a conversation first
- **Sending to wrong person** - research hiring manager/founder name
- **Waiting for perfect email** - send good one today, perfect one later
- **Ignoring feedback** - ask friends for email reviews
- **Multiple emails in a day** - space them out
- **Personal/emotional appeals** - keep professional
- **Exaggerating achievements** - be honest
- **Forgetting contact info** - always include phone and email

## Resume Optimization

### Resume Structure

```
[HEADER]
Your Name
City, State | Phone | Email | LinkedIn | Portfolio

[PROFESSIONAL SUMMARY]
Brief 2-3 line overview of your value proposition

[EXPERIENCE]
- 2-3 roles with bullets on achievements (use metrics)
- Format: "Accomplished X using Y which resulted in Z"

[SKILLS]
- Organized by category (Languages, Frameworks, Tools, etc.)
- Match keywords from job description

[EDUCATION]
- Degree, School, Graduation Year
- GPA if 3.7+ and recent graduate

[PROJECTS / PORTFOLIO]
- Optional but powerful for developers
- Include GitHub/portfolio link
```

### Achievement Format

**Bad:** "Worked on website improvements"

**Good:** "Optimized React components reducing load time by 40%, improving user retention by 25%"

**Use this structure:**
- Action verb (Built, Led, Optimized, etc.)
- What you did
- Technology/method used
- Quantifiable result

## Application Tracking Tips

### Use the Spreadsheet Effectively

1. **Set weekly goals** - "Apply to 5 companies this week"
2. **Track metrics** - Response rate, interview rate
3. **Identify patterns** - Which companies/templates work best?
4. **Review regularly** - Weekly check-in on progress
5. **Update status immediately** - Don't let it get stale
6. **Note what worked** - Reference email/resume in notes
7. **Set reminders** - Follow-up dates in calendar

## Collaboration Best Practices

### With Your Job Search Team

1. **Weekly sync** - Share updates and opportunities
2. **Email review** - Before sending, get friend to review
3. **Company research** - Divide research tasks
4. **Template feedback** - Try each other's templates
5. **Celebrate wins** - Share interviews and offers
6. **Learn from rejections** - Analyze and improve
7. **Referral hunting** - Help each other find mutual connections

## Timeline & Expectations

### First 2 Weeks
- Build profile: resumes, LinkedIn, portfolio
- Research 10-15 target companies
- Send 10-15 applications

### Week 3-6
- Expect first responses
- Response rate typically 5-10%
- Interview rate from applications: 1-3%
- Adjust strategy based on feedback

### Week 7+
- Interview scheduled or plan adjustment
- Continue applications (don't stop)
- Negotiate offers
- Keep network updated

## Red Flags in Companies

⚠️ **Be cautious if:**
- No clear job description
- Very low salary for role
- Unprofessional communication
- No benefits mentioned
- High turnover indicated
- Unclear about work location/hours

## Mental Health & Motivation

- Job search is a numbers game - expect rejections
- Celebrate small wins (interview requests, feedback)
- Take breaks - don't burnout
- Share journey with accountability partners
- Focus on learning and improvement, not just placement
- Remember: Right fit is more important than any job
